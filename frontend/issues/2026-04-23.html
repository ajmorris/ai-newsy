To be human is to live with friction. That’s something AI boosters will never understand | Alexander Hurst

# Summary and Analysis of Alexander Hurst's Article ## Key Argument Alexander Hurst argues that the push by AI proponents to eliminate all friction, inefficiency, and inconvenience from human life fundamentally misunderstands what it means to be human. He frames the current trajectory of AI-driven optimization as a dangerous extension of capitalism — what he evocatively calls "the Black Mirror stage of capitalism." ## Core Themes ### Friction as Essential to Humanity Hurst's central thesis is that the messy, inefficient, sometimes frustrating aspects of daily life — the moments of waiting, wondering, stumbling, and searching — are not bugs to be fixed but features of the human experience. These moments of friction create space for: - Reflection — pausing to think rather than being served instant answers - Spontaneity — the unplanned detours that give life richness - Serendipity — unexpected discoveries that come from not having an optimized path ### The Insomnia Anecdote His opening — lying awake wondering about the speed needed to strike a match — is a perfect illustration. That kind of idle, curious, slightly absurd wondering is quintessentially human. The impulse to immediately reach for a device and get an AI-generated answer short-circuits the very mental wandering that makes us creative, contemplative beings. ### Critique of AI Boosterism Hurst takes aim at the ideology behind AI evangelism: the assumption that removing all friction is inherently good. He suggests that AI boosters operate within a framework where efficiency is the supreme value, unable to conceive that some inefficiencies are worth preserving. ## Broader Significance This piece sits within a growing genre of techno-skeptical commentary, but Hurst's framing is distinctive. Rather than focusing on job losses, misinformation, or existential risk, he makes a phenomenological argument — that the texture of lived experience itself is at stake. The "Black Mirror stage of capitalism" phrase suggests we've moved beyond mere commodification of goods and labor into the commodification of *experience itself*, where every moment must be optimized, monetized, or streamlined. ## Critical Reflection The argument is compelling but raises questions: - Where is the line? Some friction is genuinely harmful (bureaucratic barriers to healthcare, for example). How do we distinguish between friction worth preserving and friction worth eliminating? - Nostalgia risk — Is there a danger of romanticizing inefficiency in ways that privilege those who can afford to be inefficient? - Agency — Can people choose to maintain friction in their own lives even as AI tools proliferate, or does the systemic pressure toward optimization make individual resistance futile? Hurst's piece is ultimately a plea for intentionality — for asking not just "can we optimize this?" but "should we?" — and for recognizing that a frictionless life may be a life drained of meaning.

Microsoft issues emergency update for macOS and Linux ASP.NET threat

# Microsoft Emergency Patch for ASP.NET Core Vulnerability (CVE-2026-40372) ## Summary Microsoft released an emergency patch for a critical vulnerability in ASP.NET Core's DataProtection package (CVE-2026-40372, severity 9.1/10) affecting macOS and Linux systems. The flaw allows unauthenticated attackers to gain SYSTEM-level privileges by forging authentication payloads. ## Key Details ### The Vulnerability - Affected component: `Microsoft.AspNetCore.DataProtection` NuGet package, versions 10.0.0 through 10.0.6 - Root cause: A regression bug caused the managed authenticated encryptor to compute its HMAC validation tag over the wrong bytes of the payload and then discard the computed hash - Impact: Unauthenticated attackers can forge cryptographic signatures to bypass authentication and gain full SYSTEM privileges on the underlying machine - Platforms affected: macOS, Linux, and other non-Windows operating systems (Windows is not affected because it uses different default encryptors) ### Who Is Affected Two groups of users are primarily at risk: 1. Primary group: Applications running version 10.0.6 at runtime on non-Windows OSes that either don't target `Microsoft.NET.Sdk.Web` or have a `Microsoft.AspNetCore.App` framework reference and haven't opted out of `PrunePackageReference` 2. Secondary group: Non-Windows applications using any vulnerable version that referenced the DataProtection package and consumed the `net462` or `netstandard2.0` target framework assets ### Critical Post-Patch Warning Patching alone is not sufficient. If an attacker exploited the vulnerability before the patch was applied, they could have induced the application to issue legitimately-signed tokens (session tokens, API keys, password reset links, etc.) to themselves. These forged credentials survive patching and remain valid unless the DataProtection key ring is rotated. ## Remediation Steps 1. Update immediately to `Microsoft.AspNetCore.DataProtection` version 10.0.7 2. Rotate the DataProtection key ring to invalidate any potentially forged tokens or credentials created during the vulnerable window 3. Audit logs for suspicious authentication activity during the period vulnerable versions were in use 4. Invalidate existing sessions and tokens as a precautionary measure ## Analysis This vulnerability is particularly dangerous for several reasons: - Unauthenticated exploitation: No credentials are needed to exploit it - Persistence beyond patching: Forged credentials remain valid after updating, meaning organizations that patch but don't rotate keys remain compromised - Discovery circumstances: The bug was found during investigation of decryption failures after a routine update — meaning it was introduced as a regression, highlighting the risks inherent in updates to cryptographic components - High severity (9.1/10): Reflects the combination of unauthenticated remote exploitation and full system compromise The fact that this was a regression bug — introduced in a recent update rather than a longstanding flaw — underscores the importance of rigorous testing of cryptographic code paths and the value of defense-in-depth strategies that don't rely solely on a single authentication layer.
