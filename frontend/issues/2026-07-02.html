Indian tech tycoon bets $30M of his own money to build AI alternative to Microsoft Office

Indian serial entrepreneur Bhavin Turakhia is investing $30 million of his own money into Neo, a new enterprise work platform designed from the ground up for AI, aiming to compete with Microsoft Office and Google Apps. Launched internally in April 2026, Neo combines project management, documents, file storage, and AI into a single product. Turakhia argues that existing workplace software built before the AI era cannot simply be retrofitted with chatbots and must be redesigned entirely. The platform is model-agnostic, allowing enterprises to switch between AI models. Neo was built in three months with a team of 18 engineers using AI-assisted development, and the Bengaluru-based startup plans to expand from 45 to 100 employees by year-end while targeting mid-sized businesses in technology, consulting, and professional services.

This is a deeply concerning but unsurprising finding that highlights a fundamental problem with using AI to summarize user reviews. AI summary systems tend to gravitate toward averaging sentiment, which inherently dilutes extreme negative experiences — the very experiences that are most critical for consumer safety.
