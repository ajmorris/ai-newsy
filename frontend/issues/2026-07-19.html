Claude Code Changelog

v2.1.215

Claude Code v2.1.215 changes the behavior of the /verify and /code-review skills so that Claude no longer runs them automatically on its own. Users must now explicitly invoke these skills by typing /verify or /code-review when they want them executed.

Why it matters

This is a sensible change that gives users more control over when verification and code review processes run. Automatic invocation of these skills could be disruptive or wasteful in workflows where they aren't needed, so making them opt-in is a good UX improvement. It reflects a broader principle of keeping AI assistants predictable and user-directed rather than autonomously triggering potentially expensive or time-consuming operations.

Albanese’s AI speech was a good start. Now Australia must confront these bigger questions | Julianne Schultz

The article discusses Australian Prime Minister Albanese's speech on artificial intelligence, arguing that while it was a positive step, Australia needs to address deeper questions beyond datacentres and copyright issues. Author Julianne Schultz contends that the government's AI vision must encompass capability building, national security implications, and ensuring public benefit as AI rapidly approaches ubiquity, with billions being invested globally and significant societal changes underway.
