Doctors’ soaring use of AI scribes prompts Australian government warning over privacy

The Australian federal health department has raised concerns about the rapidly growing use of AI scribes by doctors, particularly in GP surgeries. Why it matters

This is a legitimate and important concern that governments should be addressing proactively rather than reactively. Midjourney wants Hollywood studios to reveal the details of their AI usage

Midjourney is seeking to compel Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros. to reveal the full extent of their internal AI usage as part of an ongoing copyright infringement lawsuit. The studios sued Midjourney for allegedly training its image-generation models on copyrighted characters. Midjourney argues fair use and wants discovery to include all studio AI usage, not just consumer-facing outputs, claiming the studios may themselves be training AI on unlicensed copyrighted content internally. Midjourney also wants all prompts and outputs the studios used in its platform revealed, not just those producing allegedly infringing images. The studios' attorney called this a 'fishing expedition' and said they simply want Midjourney to stop copying their characters without authorization.

Why it matters

This is a fascinating legal strategy by Midjourney that could expose significant hypocrisy if the studios are indeed using AI trained on unlicensed copyrighted content internally. The argument that internal studio AI usage would establish 'industry custom' is clever and could meaningfully bolster Midjourney's fair use defense. Alibaba reportedly bans employees from using Claude Code

Alibaba has reportedly banned employees from using Anthropic's Claude Code programming tool, classifying it as high-risk software, with the ban effective July 10, 2026. Anthropic already prohibits Chinese companies from using its models and has been working to close loopholes allowing Chinese users access. A Reddit post revealed that a version of Claude Code could secretly identify Chinese users, which Anthropic's Thariq Shihipar described as an experiment launched in March to prevent account abuse from unauthorized resellers and protect against model distillation. Alibaba is directing employees to use its own Qoder tool instead.
