Anthropic’s landmark $1.5B copyright settlement is approved

A federal judge gave final approval to Anthropic's $1.5 billion class action copyright settlement with authors and book publishers. The settlement, believed to be the largest in U.S. copyright law history, will pay $3,000 per work across an estimated 500,000 works. The case originated from Judge William Alsup's ruling that while training AI on copyrighted text constitutes fair use, Anthropic illegally obtained books by downloading them from pirate sites like Library Genesis. Anthropic settled to avoid trial on the piracy question. The ruling does not set binding precedent since it was a single district court decision that won't be appealed, leaving the broader legal question of using copyrighted works for AI training unresolved as similar lawsuits continue against Google, Meta, Midjourney, and OpenAI.

Why it matters

This settlement represents a significant but incomplete milestone in AI copyright law. The $3,000 per work payout feels modest given the enormous value AI companies extract from these works, and it's understandable that many authors don't view this as a win. The most consequential aspect is Judge Alsup's fair use ruling for AI training, which, while not binding precedent, sends a strong signal to the industry.
