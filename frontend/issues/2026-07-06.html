Boost City regulator’s powers to help protect UK consumers from AI, says watchdog

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) has published a landmark review (the Mills review) examining how AI will reshape financial services from 2030 onward. The review urges ministers to strengthen the City regulator's powers to protect consumers against potential risks posed by AI, including amplified threats from cyber-crime and fraud. The review found that companies are already beginning to shift from human-led activities toward AI-driven processes, raising concerns about consumer protection in an increasingly automated financial landscape.

This is a significant and timely development. Strengthening the FCA's regulatory powers proactively, rather than waiting for a major crisis, seems like a prudent approach.
