Utility companies are promising to spare us from AI’s energy bill

Nearly 200 organizations, including major US utility companies and data center developers, have signed President Trump's 'rate payer protection pledge' aimed at ensuring average consumers don't bear the costs of AI-driven electricity demand increases. The pledge, introduced in March, includes vague commitments for AI providers to front infrastructure costs. Signatories now account for about 80% of US power delivery. However, the pledge lacks enforcement mechanisms and has done little to quell bipartisan concerns about rising electricity rates tied to AI data center expansion.

This pledge appears to be largely a PR exercise rather than a substantive policy solution. Without enforcement mechanisms, it's essentially a voluntary promise from companies whose primary obligation is to shareholders, not ratepayers. The fact that Trump himself acknowledged tech companies 'need some PR help' reveals the cynical nature of the initiative. Google also announced that pre-training for Gemini 4 has begun, calling it their 'most ambitious pre-training run yet.'
