OpenAI launches new voice intelligence features in its API

OpenAI has launched new voice intelligence features in its API, including GPT-Realtime-2 (a voice model with GPT-5-class reasoning for complex conversations), GPT-Realtime-Translate (real-time translation supporting 70+ input and 13 output languages), and GPT-Realtime-Whisper (live speech-to-text transcription). These tools are designed for developers building apps for customer service, education, media, and creator platforms. OpenAI says it has built guardrails to prevent misuse such as spam and fraud. The models are available through OpenAI's Realtime API, with Translate and Whisper billed by the minute and GPT-Realtime-2 billed by token consumption.

OpenAI introduces new ‘Trusted Contact’ safeguard for cases of possible self-harm

OpenAI announced a new optional feature called Trusted Contact that allows adult ChatGPT users to designate a friend or family member as a trusted contact. When conversations indicate possible self-harm, OpenAI's system—using automation and human review—can send an alert to the trusted contact encouraging them to check in with the user, without sharing detailed conversation content. The feature comes amid multiple lawsuits from families alleging ChatGPT encouraged or assisted in suicides. It builds on parental oversight safeguards introduced in September 2025. However, the feature is optional and users can create multiple accounts, limiting its effectiveness.

Why it matters

This is a reasonable and well-intentioned step, but its optional nature and the ease of creating multiple accounts significantly undermine its potential impact. The people most at risk of self-harm are often the least likely to proactively set up such safeguards. It feels more like a liability-mitigation measure in the face of lawsuits than a truly effective safety intervention. Perplexity’s Personal Computer is now available to everyone on Mac

Perplexity has made its 'Personal Computer' feature available to all Mac users through its new desktop app. Personal Computer brings AI agents to local devices, allowing them to access local files, native Mac apps, web tools, and over 400 connectors to handle multi-step workflows on the user's behalf. Previously limited to Max subscribers on a waitlist, it now requires only a Pro or Max subscription. The tool positions itself as a safer alternative to OpenClaw for local AI agent tasks. It can run autonomous agents on always-on devices like Mac Mini and be controlled remotely from an iPhone. Perplexity plans to deprecate its older Mac app to focus on this new one, which is currently available only as a direct download, not through the Mac App Store.
