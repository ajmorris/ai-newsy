Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis has published a proposal for a U.S.-led independent body that would safety-test advanced AI models before public release, modeled after FINRA (the financial industry's self-regulatory organization). The proposed body would screen frontier models for dangerous capabilities such as deception, bioweapons creation, and malicious hacking, with labs voluntarily submitting models for review 30 days before release. Hassabis wants the body operational before the end of 2026, warning that open-source AI capabilities could enter dangerous territory within 18 months. The proposal comes after the U.S. government's improvised handling of a recent Mythos and Fable AI ban. v2.1.209

This patch release (v2.1.209) fixes a bug where the /model command and other dialog interfaces were being blocked in `claude agents` background sessions. The fix reverts an overly broad guard that was preventing these dialogs from functioning properly in background agent sessions.

Why it matters

This is a straightforward bugfix release that addresses a regression caused by an overly broad guard condition. It's a minor but important fix for users running Claude agents in background sessions, as being unable to switch models or interact with dialogs would significantly hamper usability. The fact that it's a revert suggests the original guard was well-intentioned but had unintended side effects that weren't caught before release.
